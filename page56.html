Norepinephrine

Only known as, Levophed

  1. What Is It - It can treat low blood pressure and heart failure.

  2. How It Works - Norepinephrine increases heart rate and blood pressure, triggers the release of glucose from energy stores, increases blood flow to skeletal muscle, reduces blood flow to the gastrointestinal system, and inhibits voiding of the bladder and gastrointestinal motility. 
    Receptors: α1, α2, β1
  3.  Why It's Unique - Norepinephrine given through an IV can help to constrict blood vessels, increasing blood pressure. Although epinephrine can also be used for this purpose, norepinephrine is preferred due to its pure alpha receptor action.
