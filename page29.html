Etomidate

Also known by it's brand name, Amidate.

  1. What Is It - Used for induction of general anesthesia and also sedation for short procedures in the same ways that Propfol gets used. 
  2. How It Works - Unlike nondepolarizing drugs that bind onto a receptor and depolarizes it, drugs such as ROCuronium and VECuronium are antagonists and block receptors. This means that the normal neurotransmitter can't bind so you also get paralysis. 
  3. Why It's Unique - Propofol has a smooth rapid induction, rapid recovery, cerebro-protective effect, but causes hypotension, bradycardia, and respiratory depression pain on injection. Etomidate is a hypnotic agent causing minimal histamine releasae and very stable hemodynamic profile.
