Lorazepam

Also known as, Ativan.

  1. What Is It - It's very similar to Diazepam as it's used for sedation, anesthetic adjunct, anticonvulsant, and behavioral disorders. 
  2. How It Works - Lorazepam belongs to a group of medicines called benzodiazepines. It works by increasing the levels of a calming chemical in your brain. Depending on your health condition, this can make you feel drowsy, relieve anxiety, stop seizures or relax tense muscles.

  3.  Why It's Unique - The central difference between Lorazepam and Diazepam is Lorazepam leaves a person's system more quickly, reducing the chance of toxicity or side effects.
