Muscle Relaxants

These drugs are medications that block the nerve impulses to the muscles. They sometimes are also referred to as neuromuscular blocking agents. 
Cases that the patient is always paralyzed: Anything laparoscopic, open thorasic/abdominal cases, and transplants.
Cases that the patient is never paralyzed: LMA cases, nerve monitoring, and other simplisitc cases such as plastics, and cystos.

CISTRAcurium PANcuronium ROCuronium Succinylcholine VECuronium

Induction Drugs

Commonly used to induce general anesthesia.

Etomidate Ketamine Pentothal Propofol

Tranquilzers

While some people think sedatives and tranquilizers are indistinguisable, they are actually very different.
Sedatives, also known as depressants, slow both mentation and body functions, such as alochol. 
Tranquilizers impair nerve communication between the brain and body.

Diazepam Droperidol Lorazepam Versed

Narcotics

Opioids (narcotic analgesics) are widely used in the practice of anesthesia for pre-anesthetic medication, systemic and spinal analgesia, supplementation of general anesthetic agents, and as primary anesthesics. 

Dilaudid Fentanyl Morphine Remifentanil Sufentanil

Narcotic Antagonists

These are drugs that block euphoric (and all other) effects of opiates. Basically a narcotic reversal.

Narcan

Anticholinergic (anti-col-ingeric) Agents

Atropine Glycopyrrolate Scopolamine

Vasopressor

Vasopressor, or pressor, is any medication that tends to raise blood pressure.

Calcium Chloride Dobutamine Dopamine Ephedrine Epinephrine Norepinephrine Milrinone Phenylephrine

Hypotensive Agents

Drugs used to lower blood pressure.

Nitroglycerine Viagra

Local Anesthetics

Drugs that cause the absence of pain and/or sensation. When it is used on specific nerve pathways, paralysis also can be achieved.

Bupivacine Cocaine Lidocaine Ropivacaine

Relaxant Antagonists

A way to end the effects of neuromuscular blocking medication of the non-depolarizing type.

Neostigmine Sugammadex
