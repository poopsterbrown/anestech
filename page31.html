Propofol

Also known by it's less popular generic name, Diprivan.

  1. What Is It - No one really knows how this drug works. It's mixed in a liquid containing soybean oil and a substance called egg lecithin. It's common to be asked about egg allergies before getting propofol; however, most reports of anaphylaxis to propofol have occurred in patients without egg allergy and the vast majority of patients with egg allergy receieve propofol without any reaction.
  2. How It Works - As stated above, no one really knows, but we do know what it does. It slows the activity of your brain and nervous system. Propofol is used to help you relax before and during general anesthesia and other medical procedures. If you have enough money, you can even use it illegay as sleeping aide, although, it's not real sleep.  

  3.  Why It's Unique - Other than killing Michael Jackson, it's super fast acting, and super short lasting. It's perfect for induction, and can also be used for conscious sedation for small outpatient cases. It can also be used to treat hypertension.
