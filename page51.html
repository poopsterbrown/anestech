Calcium Chloride

Also known by, Calcium.

  1. What Is It - Calcium! It's also an inorganic compound, a salt with the chemical formula CaCl₂. It is a white coloured crystalline solid at room temperature, and it is highly soluble in water. It can be created by neutralising hydrochloric acid with calcium hydroxide. 
  2. How It Works - It can be used to increase calcium in patient's whos levels are low. Calcium chloride also makes the heart constrict (squeeze) and as a result will increase the blood pressure. Although in some EMR's Calcium chloride is catigorized as a pressor, it actually isn't, but given it's pressor capabilities, a calcium chloride injection is also used in cardiac resuscitation, arrhythmias, hypermagnesemia, calcium channel blocker overdose, and beta-blocker overdose.
